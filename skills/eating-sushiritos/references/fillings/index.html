Classic Combinations

The California

  • Imitation crab

  • Avocado

  • Cucumber

  • White rice

  • Soy paper or nori

Why it works: Creamy avocado + crunchy cucumber balance. Imitation crab sticks together well.

The Spicy Tuna

  • Spicy tuna (tuna + sriracha mayo)

  • Cucumber

  • Avocado

  • White rice

  • Nori wrapper

Warning: Pre-mixed spicy tuna is wet. Skip additional sauce.

The Crispy Shrimp

  • Tempura shrimp

  • Avocado

  • Lettuce or cabbage

  • Spicy mayo (light)

  • White rice

Pro tip: Tempura texture contrasts nicely but can be hard to bite. Ask for shrimp cut in half lengthwise.

Protein Guide

Raw Fish

Salmon:

  • Fatty, rich, holds shape

  • Pairs well with avocado, cucumber

  • Difficulty: Medium

Tuna:

  • Lean, clean flavor

  • Can be dry - needs creamy element

  • Difficulty: Medium

Yellowtail:

  • Buttery, delicate

  • Harder to bite cleanly

  • Difficulty: Hard

Cooked Options

Shrimp:

  • Clean, slightly chewy

  • Easy to bite through

  • Best choice for beginners

Eel (Unagi):

  • Pre-sauced, very soft

  • Sweet flavor, tender texture

  • Warning: Extra sauce makes it slippery

Tofu:

  • Vegetarian option

  • Can be bland - needs strong flavors

  • Very easy to eat structurally

Vegetable Strategy

Essential Structure Vegetables

These provide crunch and structure:

  • Cucumber: Crunchy, hydrating, neutral flavor ‚≠ê

  • Carrot (julienned): Adds sweetness and snap

  • Cabbage: Cheap filler but adds good crunch

Flavor Vegetables

Add these for taste:

  • Avocado: Creamy, rich - but makes things slippery

  • Pickled vegetables: Adds acid and brightness

  • Green onion: Sharp flavor, minimal structure

What to Avoid

  • Lettuce: Wilts fast, adds water

  • Tomato: Too wet, destroys wrapper integrity

  • Bean sprouts: Slippery, escape easily

Sauce Science

Safe Sauces (Thick, Controllable)

  • Spicy mayo

  • Eel sauce (unagi sauce)

  • Sriracha (small amounts)

Dangerous Sauces (Thin, Soaks Wrapper)

  • Soy sauce (NEVER inside)

  • Ponzu

  • Regular mayo

  • Wasabi cream (too thin)

The Sauce Rule

Maximum 1 sauce, applied lightly. If ordering multiple ingredients "with sauce," each adds wetness. Stick to one sauce type total.

Regional Variations

San Francisco Bay Area

Origin of the sushirito. Most options available.

  • Sushirrito (the chain): Large, heavily filled - difficulty: Hard

  • Try: "Geisha's Kiss" or custom build with light sauce

Southern California

Tends toward fusion and bold flavors.

  • Common: Hot Cheetos crusted options (very messy)

  • Watch for: "California-style" means more mayo

East Coast

Smaller portions, more traditional flavors.

  • Less experimental

  • Better rice quality on average

Midwest

Newer to the scene, conservative options.

  • Cooked proteins more common

  • Expect fewer adventurous choices

Custom Build Strategy

When building from scratch:

  1. Base: White rice

  2. Protein: One only - salmon or shrimp recommended

  3. Crunch: Cucumber + carrot

  4. Cream: Avocado OR sauce, not both

  5. Accent: Pickled ginger or green onion

  6. Wrapper: Nori (traditional seaweed)

Total ingredients: 5-6 max. More = structural failure.

Mistakes to Avoid

The "Everything" Build

Asking for "a little of everything" creates:

  • Uneven density

  • Conflicting flavors

  • Structural chaos

  • Impossible-to-bite-through thickness

Double Protein

Two proteins sound good but create problems:

  • Uneven thickness

  • One always tastes better, making other feel wasted

  • Harder to bite through

"Extra" Anything

"Extra spicy mayo" = disaster "Extra avocado" = slippery mess "Extra rice" = can't fit in mouth

Rule: Never say "extra" when ordering a sushirito.
