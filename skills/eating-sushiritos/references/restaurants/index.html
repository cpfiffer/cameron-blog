Sushirito Restaurants Guide

Popular chains, regional spots, and what to order.

Major Chains

Sushirrito (San Francisco Bay Area)

The original. Founded in San Francisco, 2011.

Locations: SF, Oakland, Berkeley, Palo Alto, and expanding

What to order:

  • Geisha's Kiss: Salmon, tuna, yellowtail, soy paper - expert level

  • Sumo Crunch: Tempura shrimp, cucumber, avocado - beginner friendly ‚≠ê

  • Go Veg: Tofu, lots of vegetables - easiest to eat

Pro tip: Order online to skip the line. They're always busy at lunch.

Difficulty rating: Hard - these are LARGE (1+ lb) and heavily filled

Pokeworks (National chain)

Primarily poke bowls, but many locations offer sushi burritos.

Locations: 100+ across US

What to order:

  • Build-your-own is best

  • Stick to their "signature" builds - staff knows how to balance them

Pro tip: Smaller than Sushirrito, better for beginners.

Difficulty rating: Medium

Buredo (New York)

East Coast answer to Sushirrito.

Locations: NYC area

What to order:

  • The Jade: Spicy salmon, cucumber, avocado

  • The Dragon: Eel, avocado, cucumber

Note: Smaller portions, more traditional flavors, easier to handle.

Difficulty rating: Easy-Medium

Ordering Tips by Restaurant Type

Fast-Casual Chains (Pokeworks, Poke Bar, etc.)

  • Build-your-own is the move

  • Staff are used to helping - ask questions

  • Specify "light" on sauces always

  • Peak hours = assembly line quality drops

Best time: 2-4 PM (between lunch and dinner rush)

Dedicated Sushi Burrito Shops (Sushirrito, Buredo)

  • Signature rolls are tested and balanced

  • Custom builds can get expensive

  • These make the biggest burritos - come hungry

  • Always busy - order ahead if possible

Best time: Order online for pickup

Traditional Sushi Restaurants with Burrito Option

  • Usually highest quality fish

  • Smaller portions, higher price

  • Staff may not be experienced with burrito format

  • Sometimes use too much rice

Pro tip: Order a hand roll instead - same concept, better execution

Ordering for Delivery

Packaging Matters

Sushiritos delivered poorly = disaster

What to ask:

  • How is it packaged? (Should be in rigid container, not just wrapped)

  • How long is delivery? (Under 20 mins ideal, over 30 mins risky)

  • Can I get sauce on the side? (Always yes to this)

Delivery Tips

  • Order from closest location only

  • Avoid peak hours (rice quality suffers when rushed)

  • Eat immediately upon arrival - nori gets soggy fast

  • Consider ordering a poke bowl instead for delivery (holds up better)

Harsh truth: Sushiritos are best eaten fresh in-store. Delivery is always a compromise.

Making Friends with Staff

Good Customer Behavior

  • Know what you want before reaching the counter

  • Don't ask for "a little bit of everything"

  • Tip well if you're building custom

  • Off-peak hours = staff can give better recommendations

Questions That Help

  • "What's most popular?"

  • "What's fresh today?"

  • "Can you go light on the sauce?"

  • "What do you recommend for a first-timer?"

Questions That Annoy

  • "Can I taste everything first?"

  • "Can you make it like [describes complex custom thing]?"

  • "Why is there an upcharge for [premium ingredient]?"

Pro tip: Staff who make sushiritos all day know the optimal builds. Trust their recommendations.
